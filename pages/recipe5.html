One Pot Spinach Mushroom Pasta

Craving a comforting and flavorful vegetarian meal that comes together in a single pot? Look no further than this one-pot spinach mushroom pasta. This recipe is incredibly easy to make, requiring minimal prep and just one pot for cooking. It's also packed with nutrients and flavor, making it a perfect choice for a quick and satisfying weeknight dinner.

The base of this dish is built on a simple combination of olive oil, onion, and garlic. Saut√©ing these ingredients together creates a flavorful base that infuses the entire dish. Next, we add sliced mushrooms, which cook down and release their earthy flavor. You can use any type of mushroom you like, such as cremini mushrooms, portobello mushrooms, or a combination for added variety.

For a pop of color and freshness, we incorporate spinach. Spinach cooks down quickly and wilts easily, adding a touch of bitterness that balances the other flavors in the dish. You can use fresh spinach or frozen spinach for this recipe. If using frozen spinach, be sure to thaw and squeeze out any excess moisture before adding it to the pot.

Finally, we add the pasta, vegetable broth, and Parmesan cheese. The vegetable broth adds depth and body to the sauce, while the Parmesan cheese provides a salty and nutty flavor that ties everything together. The pasta cooks directly in the pot with all the other ingredients, eliminating the need for a separate pot of boiling water.

Once the pasta is cooked through and the sauce has thickened slightly, your one-pot spinach mushroom pasta is ready to serve. Top it with additional Parmesan cheese, red pepper flakes, or a drizzle of olive oil for extra flavor and enjoy!
